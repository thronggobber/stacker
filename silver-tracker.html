Buying rules

  • Compare cost per ounce, not sticker price. The Premium tab does this.
  • Card and PayPal usually cost 3-4% more than wire, ACH, or check. That difference is a whole premium tier.
  • Spot moves. The price that matters is at checkout, not the listing you saw this morning.
  • Discounts kick in at tubes (20 oz) and again at 100+ oz. Save quotes here to compare tiers.
  • Skip graded modern bullion. An MS70 sticker on a bullion coin is a premium you never get back.
  • Lowest premium builds the stack fastest. Eagles and 90% resell easiest. Balance both.
  • Take delivery insured with signature required. Log every lot on the Stack tab same day, with cost and date.

Verifying metal

  • Weight and dimensions first. A caliper and 0.01 g scale catch most fakes. Compare against mint specs.
  • Silver is not magnetic, but a strong neodymium magnet should slide slowly down a tilted bar (eddy current drag).
  • Ping test: real silver rings with a long chime. Dull thud is a problem.
  • Ice cube melts visibly faster on silver than on anything else its size (thermal conductivity).
  • Buying serious volume, get a Sigma Metalytics verifier. Non-destructive and fast.

Records & selling

  • Your cost basis lives on the Stack tab. Export a backup after every purchase; that is your record when you sell.
  • Dealers buy generic near or below spot, Eagles and 90% closer to retail when demand is hot. Check buyback prices before choosing what to stack.
  • Physical silver is a collectible for US federal capital gains, taxed up to 28% on long-term gains. Some bulk sales trigger dealer 1099-B reporting. Not tax advice, just the shape of it.
